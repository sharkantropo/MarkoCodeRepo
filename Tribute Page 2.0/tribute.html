Ultima is a serie of Open World Rpgs developed by Richard Garriott. Many innovations of the early Ultimas – in particular Ultima III: Exodus (1983) onward– eventually became standard among the RPG genre, such as the use of tiled graphics and party-based combat, its mix of fantasy and science-fiction elements, and the introduction of time travel as a plot device. In turn, some of these elements were inspired by Wizardry, specifically the party-based combat. Exodus was also revolutionary in its use of a written narrative to convey a larger story than the typically minimal plots that were common at the time; also, Quest of the Avatar deconstructs the typical narrative about vanquishing a major evil by setting the player in a quest to reach an state of enlightenment known as "Avatar of virtues", which is reached after solving a series of puzzles and reading a book deep within an ominous place called the Stygian Abyss.
